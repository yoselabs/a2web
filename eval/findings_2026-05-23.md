## What was tested for the change

The change targets four issues from prior eval:

1. **linux.do CF block** — fixed by Phase 1 (curl_cffi-impersonated
   `fetch_bytes`). Validated by `make handler-probe`: linux.do passes
   1093ms, 2429 chars rendered. Not exercised by the bench corpus, which
   has no Discourse URL.
2. **Discourse title `&rsquo;`** — fixed by Phase 2 (`html_fragment.to_text`
   on `fancy_title`). Pinned by `test_render_topic_decodes_html_entities_in_fancy_title`.
   Not exercised by the bench corpus.
3. **Noisy generic-record content_md** — fixed by Phase 4
   (`heading_text` + `heading_link`, renderer leads with
   `[title](href)`). **Not exercised by this bench corpus either** — every
   listing URL in the corpus (`hn-front`, `gh-trending`, `reddit-listing`,
   `medium-tag`) routes through a site-handler and bypasses
   `record_extract`. The improvement is pinned by
   `test_record_markdown_leads_with_heading_link_then_body_without_duplication`;
   to see the effect at the bench level, the corpus needs a no-handler
   aggregator like `lobste.rs/active`.

## Where things look stable

- **Quality:** a2web still beats WebFetch on average (a2web_detail
  4.10 / a2web_extract 3.90 vs 3.50). WebFetch climbed 0.60 day-on-day —
  judge variance, not a regression.
- **Clarity:** `a2web_extract` 3.75 holds the lead. `a2web_detail`
  clarity remains poor (1.11) — same finding as the baseline; full-page
  markdown is hard to act on, `ask` should be the default.
- **Data contract:** 10/10 conformant, both systems, both debug modes.
  No envelope drift from the v0.14 deviation/debug rules.
- **next_links:** `a2web_extract` improved 3.67 → 4.67. Possibly noise;
  possibly the `heading_link` rename surfacing better source candidates
  for the few corpus URLs that fall through to `record_extract` after a
  handler misses.

## Where things still need work

- **Comment threads, `a2web_extract`:** comments class dropped 2.5 → 2.5
  (extract) but a2web_detail recovered to 4.50. Reddit's anti-bot lockout
  continues to cap that cell — same issue as the baseline, no change.
- **`a2web_detail` envelope inflation** continues — 3760 mean tokens
  vs 2554 at baseline. The detail mode is meant as a fallback; the bench
  keeps confirming it's expensive without being clearer.

## Add to corpus (follow-up)

The most useful next bench addition is a no-handler aggregator that
exercises `record_extract`:

- `lobste.rs/active` (or `/recent`) — small Rails forum, no site-handler,
  fits the listing class. Would empirically demonstrate the Phase 4
  `[title](url)\\n  meta` format vs the prior flat smush.
