The article describes using Simulink and Multibody to design a PID-controlled inverted pendulum system without requiring knowledge in physics, mathematics, or engineering. It explains how to assemble and configure the model with automatic tuning of PID controllers for stabilizing both the pendulum's angle and cart position. The process includes adding noise and a Kalman filter for realism and robustness, resulting in a stable simulation that can be further simplified by using only two PID controllers.
