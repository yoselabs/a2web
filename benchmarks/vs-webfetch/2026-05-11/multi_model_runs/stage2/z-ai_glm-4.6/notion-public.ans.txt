
A Notion database is "a collection of pages" that helps you "manage and organize multiple pages in one place." Unlike regular pages, databases have special features:

- Each database item is its own Notion page that can be opened and edited
- You can "customize properties" with dates, status, and links
- Data can be visualized in multiple formats (table, list, calendar, etc.)
- Multiple data sets can be managed within a single database

The key difference is that while a regular page is standalone content, a database is a structured system for organizing related pages with customizable properties and views.
