Rust was originally designed by Graydon Hoare in 2006, with its first stable release occurring in May 2015. It is known for its memory-safety mechanism called "borrow checking," which tracks object lifetimes of references at compile time to prevent memory safety errors and data races without a conventional garbage collector.

- **Designer**: Graydon Hoare
- **First Stable Release**: May 2015
- **Memory-Safety Mechanism**: Borrow checker
