## What is a Notion Database?

A Notion database is a collection of pages that helps you manage and organize multiple items in one place. The key distinction from a regular Notion page is that databases are designed specifically for managing structured, organized data across multiple entries.

## Key Differences from Regular Notion Pages

**1. Multiple Items as Pages**
- Every item in a database is its own Notion page that you can fully customize with text, images, and other content
- A regular page is typically a single document

**2. Customizable Properties**
- Databases let you add properties (like dates, status, links) to contextualize and label each item
- Regular pages don't have this structured property system

**3. Multiple Visualization Options**
- Databases can be viewed in different formats: table, list, calendar, chart, gallery, and board views
- Regular pages display content in a single linear format

**4. Interconnected Data**
- You can link multiple databases together and view data from across your workspace in one place
- For example, you could have contacts, companies, and deals all in one CRM database while treating them as separate data sources
- Regular pages don't have this relational capability

**5. Structured Organization**
- Databases support views, filters, sorting, and grouping to organize data in different ways
- Regular pages require manual organization of content

In essence, databases are Notion's tool for structured data management, while regular pages are better suited for narrative content and free-form information.
