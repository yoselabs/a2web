A **Notion database** is a collection of pages designed to organize and manage multiple items in one place. According to the documentation, databases are one of Notion's fundamental features and differ from regular pages in several key ways:

**Key characteristics of databases:**

1. **Every item is its own page** — Each database entry (row, card, etc.) is a full Notion page that you can open, expand, and add any content to (text, images, etc.)

2. **Customizable properties** — You can add structured data fields (properties) like dates, status, links, and other attributes to contextualize and organize items

3. **Multiple visualization options** — Your data can be displayed as a table, list, calendar, chart, gallery, board, or other views — not just one fixed format

4. **Multiple data sources in one place** — You can link and display data from different parts of your workspace in a single database (e.g., a CRM database managing contacts, companies, and deals together)

**Contrast with a regular page:**
A regular Notion page is a single document with free-form content. A database, by contrast, is a structured collection where each item has consistent properties and can be organized, filtered, sorted, and viewed in multiple ways.
